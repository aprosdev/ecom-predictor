Official Contributions Program Launch
We are excited to share a new feature that will give vendors a greater voice with their customers. Starting today, you will be able to answer questions and comment on reviews in an official capacity.

Amazon will ask you to answer customer questions submitted through the Customer Questions & Answers section on your product detail pages. Your answers will be marked with a "Manufacturer" badge to mark it as an official answer. On product reviews, comments made by you will also be marked with the "Manufacturer" badge. Additionally, you will have access to reports in Vendor Central under the Reports tab that track questions and reviews for your products.

To get started, give a non-admin Vendor Central user the "Customer Interaction" permission. After you have the right, you'll start receiving emails with questions to answer. It is important that you answer using the link in the email to ensure the manufacturer badge is provided. You won't be asked to answer every question, and it's not mandatory that you answer questions. We recommend creating a public name in your profile that corresponds to your business. 

All answers and comments are subject to moderation and our general posting guidelines. Be informative, specific, and respectful when posting comments on reviews. It should add value to the review in a way that helps future customers make shopping decisions. Simply thanking the customer for the review is not allowed.

For more information on this program and how to get started, please refer to the Official Contributions FAQs document in the Vendor Central Resource Center. 

Thank you for being part of the Amazon community. We look forward to your continued engagement.

New Operational Performance Dashboard
An Operational Performance Dashboard is now live in Vendor Central. This brand new functionality will serve as a one-stop-shop giving you the actionable data and tools you need to eliminate performance issues from your operations. You can find the new Operational Performance Dashboard under Reports > Operational Performance.

The new Operational Performance Dashboard in Vendor Central:
  • Rates your operational performance health and provides the factors impacting it.
  • Displays your operational performance metrics against Amazon’s targets, as well as the financial impact of non-compliance.
  • Enables you to find the root cause of non-compliance issues so that you can take action.
  • Includes tips and alerts to help you prevent future non-compliance issues and associated charges.

Please note:
  • Our chargeback and performance management policies are not changing with the launch of the Operational Performance Dashboard.
  • The defect-related operational performance metrics that used to appear in Amazon Retail Analytics Basic can now be found in the new Operational Performance Dashboard. The other metrics are no longer available.
  • The Vendor Operational Performance page under “Payments” tab will still be available on Vendor Central for the time being. The information in that page will continue to be updated.

Please explore the dashboard and take the feature tour in order to become familiar with it and to start benefiting from it.

We hope that you like this new feature and use it effectively to manage your performance.  Please use the “Rate this page” feature to let us know if you find the new Dashboard useful.

ARA Basic Metric Deprecation and Migration

On October 15, 2016, the ARA-Basic and the Vendor Performance Dashboard will be migrating a set of metrics from the Operational Metrics reports to the Vendor Performance Dashboard. The sections "Receiving" and "Inbound defects" will be removed from the Operational Metrics reports. The reports "Inbound defects," "Purchase Orders Shipment," and "Purchase Orders Items Details Shipment" will be removed from ARA Basic as well.

The metrics being migrated to the Vendor Performance Dashboard are:

Section "Purchasing":

  • On-time fulfillment rate
  • On-time (non-backorder)
  • Late received units
  • Early received units

Section "Receiving":

  • Units received
  • ASN Units
  • ASN coverage rate
  • ASN Late Quantity
  • ASN Missing Quantity
  • ASN Parameters Incorrect Quantity
  • Inaccurate Ship Notification Quantity

Section "Inbound defects":

  • Units Not on POs
  • Units over requested quantity
  • Units sent to incorrect fulfillment center
  • Percent units sent to incorrect fulfillment center
  • Unconfirmed Quantity
  • Unconfirmed Rate
  • Problem Receive Units (all reasons)

Starting October 15, 2016, you'll be able to access these migrated metrics here.

Also, the following metrics will be deprecated from the Operational Metrics report and will no longer be available:

Section "Lead Times":

  • Overall lead time StdDev
  • Submit to confirm Std Dev
  • Prep time StdDev (days)

Important Change to Customer Review Policy

Amazon no longer permits providing free or discounted products in exchange for product reviews. You can read more about this change here. Please note that this policy is effective immediately, and the policy applies regardless of whether you provide compensation to reviewers directly or through a third party. If you continue to offer free or discounted products in exchange for a review, your Amazon privileges may be suspended or revoked.

You may continue to offer discounts and promotions as long as they are not offered in exchange for reviews.

You can find the updated Community Guidelines here.
